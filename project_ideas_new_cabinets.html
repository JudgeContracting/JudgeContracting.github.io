Getting New Cabinets

Are you thinking about new cabinets for the kitchen? Bathroom? Or other space? This article will offer some quick advice on things to keep in mind when considering new cabinets.

The Terminology

Prefabricated Cabinets

These are cabinets that are manufactured in specific sizes before being sold. They are also known as 'Box' cabinets or 'Stock' cabinets. These feature fast turn around time and lower project costs but come with a limited range in style and size.

Custom Cabinets

Custom cabinets are built according the specifications of both the physical dimensionality of the space they will be placed in as well as the specifications of what the client wants out of their cabinets (in terms of style and functionality).

Custom cabinets will fit both the needs and desires of a project outcome far more than prefabricated cabinets will. However, custom cabinetry takes a little more time for delivery as they are made-to-order. They can also incur more project expense than prefabricated cabinets depending on the nature of the customization.

What to Consider

What don't you like about your current cabinets (or lack of cabinets)?

It is very important that before you head out on the journey of replacing old cabinets or just adding in new ones, that you completely specify what it is you want to be the outcomes of the project.

You may need more storage space. You may want the cabinet layout to be redone for reasons of functionality, etc. You may just want the cabinets to have a fresh new look. You may need to replace aging and deteriorating cabinets.

Sometimes, if style is the only concern a face-lift instead of a replacement project is needed. A face-lift is where the old cabinets are still used but only parts of them are replaced or updated in some way. For example, maybe only the doors on the cabinets can be replaced to give it a fresh new look. Or perhaps it only needs a new paint or finish to give it the right feel you want. Other times, the project may be much simpler and only knob/pull replacements are desired or needed.

However, it is just as common for cabinet layouts to have been initially poorly designed. It could be that the cabinet layout in a particular space (such as the Kitchen) may lack in being cooperative with your routine. It is very possible for cabinets to be too much in the way as well as too far out of the way in terms of ease of access. If this is the case, it can be very useful to consider a custom cabinetry project to replace the current layout of cabinets you have. That may seem like a lot of effort, but the benefits are long-term and should be viewed as an appropriate investment into how a space (home, office, etc.) works for you.

What do you want the new cabinets to bring-to-the-table?

Once, you have determined what it is that you do not like about your current cabinets. Be sure to also list out the things you want your new cabinets to absolutely have. Make sure to list the things most important to you as the final outcome of the project.

Consider the purpose of your investment. Will this be for personal use or enjoyment? Or is this an update to a home or other space for resell?

Conclusions

Cabinets don't have to break the bank or be over-stressful. Once you have done your homework you should feel comfortable in your choices. We hope that this article helped you towards that!
