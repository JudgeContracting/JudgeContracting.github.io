Wood Flooring

Wood flooring in your future? There are many types to choose from. There is the true solid plank strip flooring (not so much a DIY job). There are laminate glue down floor systems that give the linear look of true solid plank flooring. There are also floating floor systems that do not require gluing or nailing. You can also choose from many types of parquet (patterned flooring).

Buying the right type of new wood flooring takes patience. There are many factors that you need to consider.

You need to determine the style and look that you are after. For example, dark grains often come across as warm, whereas lighter shades can help create a sense of space. Be careful when making your choice, it is good to remember that wood flooring is a lifetime proposition.

Remember when choosing your wood flooring that all systems are not of the same quality. Laminate and MDF systems are not as durable as traditional plank flooring. While laminate and MDF systems come with a manufacturers finish, traditional flooring leaves the choice of finish up to you allowing flexibility in the areas of color and luster.

No matter your choice in floor systems, to get the greatest durability from your flooring you should maintain your wood floor with periodic paste-waxing. Try to avoid damp mopping your wood floors.

If you do your homework [0], you should end up with something that is perfect for your home and is well worth the money that is spent.
